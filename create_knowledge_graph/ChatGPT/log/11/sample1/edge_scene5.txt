Alice, Fish-Footman, observe, For a minute or two she stood looking at the house, and wondering what to do next, when suddenly a footman in livery came running out of the wood.
Fish-Footman, Duchess, deliver message, The Fish-Footman began by producing from under his arm a great letter, nearly as large as himself, and this he handed over to the other, saying, in a solemn tone, “For the Duchess. An invitation from the Queen to play croquet.”
Frog-Footman, Duchess, deliver message, The Frog-Footman repeated, in the same solemn tone, only changing the order of the words a little, “From the Queen. An invitation for the Duchess to play croquet.”
Alice, Footman, converse, Alice went timidly up to the door, and knocked.
Duchess, Baby, nurse, The Duchess was sitting on a three-legged stool in the middle, nursing a baby.
Cook, Duchess, Baby, throw objects, The cook took the cauldron of soup off the fire, and at once set to work throwing everything within her reach at the Duchess and the baby.
Duchess, Cheshire Cat, own, “It’s a Cheshire cat,” said the Duchess.
Alice, Duchess, converse, “But what am I to do?” said Alice.
Cheshire Cat, Alice, converse, “Cheshire Puss,” she began, rather timidly, as she did not at all know whether it would like the name:
Cheshire Cat, Hatter, indicate direction, “In that direction,” the Cat said, waving its right paw round, “lives a Hatter: and in that direction,” waving the other paw, “lives a March Hare.
Cheshire Cat, March Hare, indicate direction, “In that direction,” the Cat said, waving its right paw round, “lives a March Hare. Visit either you like: they’re both mad.”
Duchess, Alice, converse, Alice caught the baby with some difficulty, as it was a queer-shaped little creature, and held out its arms and legs in all directions.
