Mr. Dance, Jim Hawkins, instruct and accompany, Mr. Dance told me to jump down and knock, and taking me along with him, was admitted.
The squire, Dr. Livesey, sit with, discuss, express intention, and agree to secrecy, the squire and Dr. Livesey sat, pipe in hand; discuss Flint and the possibility of his treasure; The squire exclaimed his plans to set sail for the treasure; “Livesey,” returned the squire, “you are always in the right of it. I’ll be as silent as the grave.”
Dr. Livesey, Jim Hawkins, question, inquire, hand over, and offer protection, “And good evening to you, friend Jim. What good wind brings you here?”; “And so, Jim,” said the doctor, “you have the thing that they were after, have you?”; “Here it is, sir,” said I, and gave him the oilskin packet.; Jim and I shall stick together in the meanwhile.
The squire, Jim Hawkins, instruct, compliment, promise adventure, and express caution, The squire said “Hawkins, will you ring that bell? Mr. Dance must have some ale.”; “As you will, Livesey,” said the squire; “Hawkins has earned better than cold pie.”; Hawkins shall come as cabin-boy.; you’ll take Joyce and Hunter when you ride to Bristol.
